Ricky Williams

Ricky Williams against the Jets

Errick Lynne Williams, was born May 21, 1977. Growing up through humble beginnings in San Diego, where he excelled in football, baseball, and track. At a very young age, he had an intuitive sense that life is an adventure, and that the highs and lows are therefore expected (and welcomed) as part of the ride. With this understanding, he was able to harness the focus needed to rise to the top on the athletic field while weathering the inevitable storms of life. His performance as a running back at Patrick Henry High School led to his recruitment by the University of Texas, where he had a record-breaking career and was a two All-American.


  • 1983: Parents Divorced, and at the age of five was taking care of his sisters by putting them to bed and even cooking for them.
  • 1994: He rushed for a total of 4,129 yards and 55 touchdowns, and in his senior season he ran for 2,099 yards and 25 touchdowns. Earning the San Diego Union-Tribune's 1994 Player of the Year award.
  • 1995: Williams accepted an athletic scholarship to attend the University of Texas, where he played for the Texas Longhorns football team from 1995 to 1998. Williams holds or shares 20 NCAA records, and became the NCAA Division I-A career rushing leader in 1998 with 6,279 yards
  • 1998: Was awarded the 64th Heisman Trophy, at the age of 21
  • 1999: Williams was selected in the first round as the fifth pick by the New Orleans Saints. In a daring attempt by the organization to trade away all draft picks for williams.
  • 2000-2001: Williams rushed for 1,000 yards and scored nine touchdowns
  • 2002: Williams was traded to the Miami Dolphins, for four draft picks, including two first-round picks. His first season with the Dolphins, he was the NFL's leading rusher with 1,853 yards, a First-team All-Pro and a Pro Bowler.
  • 2004: Rumored to have failed a third drug test before announcing his retirement, Williams made his retirement official on August 2, 2004. Williams was ineligible to play for the 2004 season, and studied Ayurveda, the ancient Indian system of holistic medicine, at the California College of Ayurveda that autumn in Grass Valley, California.
  • 2005: Williams officially returned to the Dolphins on July 24, 2005, paid back a percentage of his signing bonus and completed his four-game suspension for a failed drug test.
  • 2006: Williams was suspended for the entire 2006 season. It has been suggested that the substance may have been an herb related to his interest in holistic medicine.
  • 2006: Williams signed to the CFL Toronto Argonauts. Williams rushed 109 times for 526 yards, scoring two touchdowns, with a long run of 35 yards. He caught 19 passes for 127 yards.
  • 2007: Due to the circumstances that followed Williams and his signing to a CFL team during his NFL suspension, CFL comissioner introduced a new rule that would prevent a player under suspension in the NFL from signing with a CFL club. It was dubbed "The Ricky Williams Rule."
  • 2007: Reinstated by commissioner Roger Goodell, with a short stint during Monday Night Football.
  • 2008: Williams bounced back in the 2008 season, rushing for 659 yards on 160 carries and scoring four touchdowns while playing in all 16 games for the Dolphins.
  • 2009: during the Dolphins ninth game, starting running back Ronnie Brown suffered a season-ending injury,[33] and Williams, at age 32, became the starter for the remainder of the year. He finished the season with 1,121 yards rushing and a 4.7 yards per carry, along the way setting an NFL record of the longest time span (six years) between 1,000 yard seasons (2003â€“2009). Williams also became only the seventh player in NFL history to begin a season age 32 or older and reach 1,000 yards rushing during that season.
  • 2011:Williams signed a two-year, $2.5 million contract with the Baltimore Ravens
  • 2012: Williams surpassed the 10,000 career rushing yards mark and became the 26th player in the history of the NFL to do so. He also announced his official retirement.
